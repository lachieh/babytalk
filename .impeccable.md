## Design Context

### Users

Exhausted new parents — often sleep-deprived, frequently one-handed (holding a baby), logging events during nighttime feedings or hectic moments. They need zero-friction interaction: voice-first input, large touch targets reachable by thumb, and an interface that works in low-light conditions. The job to be done is tracking baby events (feeds, sleep, diapers) without it feeling like work — "relief, not software."

### Brand Personality

**Minimal, smart, serene.** The app should feel like a calm, competent companion that anticipates needs and gets out of the way. It never overwhelms, never judges, and never adds cognitive load. The tone is quietly confident — intelligent without being clinical, warm without being saccharine.

### Aesthetic Direction

**Soft & organic** — rounded shapes, warm neutrals, gentle transitions, and generous whitespace. Think the serenity of Calm/Headspace married with the premium data presentation of Apple Health/Oura. Avoid sharp edges, harsh contrasts, and dense layouts. The interface should feel like a deep breath.

**References:**

- Apple Health / Oura — premium health tracking that's data-rich but beautiful and approachable
- Calm / Headspace — soothing, wellness-oriented, stress-reducing

**Anti-references:**

- Generic "AI app" aesthetics — cyan-on-dark, Inter font everywhere, purple gradients, glassmorphism
- Cluttered baby apps with cartoon illustrations and overstimulating colors
- Dense dashboards that demand attention instead of offering calm

**Color direction:**

- Warm neutrals as the foundation (not cold grays)
- Soft, muted accent colors for event types (feed, sleep, diaper) — not saturated primaries
- Green/yellow/red urgency spectrum for timers (already spec'd), but softened to match the organic feel
- Auto dark mode (8pm-7am) — dark palette should feel cozy, not stark

**Typography direction:**

- Clean, humanist sans-serif — readable at small sizes, warm at large sizes
- Clear hierarchy: calm headings, comfortable body text, legible captions
- Generous line-height for scannability during bleary-eyed moments

### Design Principles

1. **One-hand, one-glance** — Every interaction must be completable with one thumb. Every screen must communicate its state in under 2 seconds. Parents don't have spare hands or attention.

2. **Calm over clever** — Prefer stillness to animation, whitespace to content, and silence to notification. Motion is purposeful (confirmation feedback, state transitions), never decorative.

3. **Soft precision** — Organic and warm doesn't mean sloppy. Maintain rigorous alignment, consistent spacing, and intentional typography. The softness is in the palette and shapes, not in the craft.

4. **Context-aware kindness** — The interface adapts to the moment: darker at night, quieter during sleep tracking, celebratory for milestones. It reads the room.

5. **Invisible infrastructure** — Syncing, offline mode, voice processing — all of it should be invisible. Never show loading spinners when you can show optimistic UI. Never ask the user to wait.

### Accessibility

- WCAG AA compliance as baseline (contrast ratios, keyboard navigation, screen reader support)
- One-handed use priority: all primary actions within thumb reach zone, minimum 44px touch targets
- Low-light optimization: dark mode designed for 3am feeding sessions, not just inverted colors
- Reduced motion respected: all animations behind prefers-reduced-motion checks
- Event type identification never relies on color alone (icons + labels alongside color coding)
